Know More About Liver Disease


What is liver Disease?

The liver is a vital organ that performs many important functions, including:

  • Filtering toxins from the blood

  • Producing bile for digestion

  • Storing vitamins and minerals

  • Regulating blood sugar levels.

  • Producing proteins necessary for blood clotting

Liver disease refers to any condition that damages the liver or affects its ability to function properly.




Types of Liver Disease


1. Fatty Liver Disease


  • Fat accumulates in liver cells.

  • Can be caused by obesity, diabetes, or alcohol consumption.

2. Hepatitis


  • Inflammation of the liver caused by viruses, alcohol, toxins, or autoimmune conditions.

  • Types include Hepatitis A, B, C, D, and E.

3. Liver Cirrhosis


  • Severe scarring of liver tissue.

  • Often results from chronic hepatitis or long-term alcohol use.

4. Liver Cancer


  • Uncontrolled growth of abnormal cells in the liver.

5. Acute Liver Failure


  • Sudden loss of liver function that requires immediate medical attention.


What is Fatty Liver Disease?

Fatty Liver Disease is a condition in which excess fat builds up in the liver cells. Normally, the liver contains a small amount of fat, but when fat makes up more than about 5–10% of the liver's weight, it is considered fatty liver disease. Fatty liver disease can range from simple fat accumulation to inflammation and liver damage, which may eventually lead to cirrhosis or liver failure if left untreated.

Types of Fatty Liver Disease


1. Non-Alcoholic Fatty Liver Disease (NAFLD)


  • Occurs in people who drink little or no alcohol.

  • Commonly associated with obesity, diabetes, and high cholesterol.

2. Alcohol-Related Fatty Liver Disease


  • Caused by excessive alcohol consumption.

  • Often improves if alcohol intake is stopped.

Common Symptoms of Liver Disease


Liver disease can develop gradually, and symptoms may not appear until significant liver damage has occurred. Common symptoms include:




  • Fatigue and weakness

  • Loss of appetite

  • Nausea and vomiting

  • Abdominal pain

  • Yellowing of the skin and eyes (jaundice)

  • Dark-colored urine

  • Pale stools

  • Swelling in the legs and abdomen

The Ayurvedic Correlation: Yakrit Roga & Meda Dhatu Imbalance


Yakrit Roga (Liver Disorders)

In Ayurveda, the liver is known as Yakrit. Diseases affecting the liver are collectively referred to as Yakrit Roga. The liver plays a vital role in digestion, metabolism, blood formation, and detoxification. Any disturbance in these functions can lead to liver disorders. Ayurvedic texts associate liver diseases primarily with an aggravation of Pitta Dosha, especially when digestive fire (Agni) becomes impaired and toxins (Ama) accumulate in the body.

  • Meda Dhatu Imbalance: When Meda Dhatu becomes excessive or improperly metabolized due to poor digestion and lifestyle habits, fat accumulates in different tissues, including the liver. This condition closely resembles modern Fatty Liver Disease.

  • Ama Pachana (Removal of Toxins): Ama Pachana is an important Ayurvedic treatment principle that refers to the digestion, neutralization, and elimination of Ama (toxins) from the body. According to Ayurveda, Ama is formed when food is not properly digested due to weak digestive fire (Agni).

  • Mandagni: Mandagni is an Ayurvedic term that means weak or sluggish digestive fire. In Ayurveda, Agni (digestive fire) is responsible for digesting food, absorbing nutrients, and maintaining metabolism. When Agni becomes weak, it is called Mandagni.
